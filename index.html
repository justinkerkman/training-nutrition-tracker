Foundations + Power Weapons · ~50 min
Warm-up — 15 min
Cross-trainer + mobility + shadow boxing
10 min cross-trainer · 3 min mobility · 2 min loose shadow boxing
▼

Technique Rounds
5 × 3 min · 1 min rest between rounds
~20 min total
▼
  • Round 1 — Jab-Cross FoundationJab-cross, jab-cross-hook — snap punches back fast. Mix in teep to reset distance.
  • Round 2 — Leg Kick CombosJab-cross-rear leg kick. Switch stance, lead leg kick. Guard UP after every kick.
  • Round 3 — Knees & Clinch WorkWalk bag into corner, grab → 10 alternating knees → push off → jab-cross → repeat. Simulate clinch entry and exit.
  • Round 4 — Elbows & Short CombosJab-cross-lead elbow. Cross-hook-rear elbow. Step INTO the bag — close range, drive through.
  • Round 5 — Mixed Weapons, Free FlowNo pattern. Punches, kicks, elbows, knees. React to bag swing. At least one kick per combo.

~8 min total
▼

  • Rear teep holdSlow & controlled · 30 s each leg · 5 s rest
  • Burnout roundMax output, anything goes · 60 s work · 60 s rest
Repeat all three × 3 blocks total.

Technique Rounds
5 × 3 min · 1 min rest between rounds
~20 min total
▼
  • Round 1 — Switch KicksFrom orthodox: quick hop to switch feet, fire left shin into bag. Tiny sharp hop, not a jump. Hip rotation = power. Hands UP.
  • Round 2 — Defensive Combos (Slip & Counter)Slip outside the jab → cross-hook-kick. Slip inside → lead uppercut-cross-rear kick. Visualise the opponent. Move head BEFORE you counter.
  • Round 3 — Spinning Back ElbowJab-cross to close → pivot on lead foot → spin → drive rear elbow across. Chin tucked through the spin. Slow first; speed follows technique.
  • Round 4 — Jumping Knees & Clinch PowerGrab bag, jump and drive both knees up alternately × 8 reps. Release, jab-cross-hook, re-clinch — repeat. Land soft, stay on balls of feet.
  • Round 5 — Pressure Fighter (Cut Angles)After every combo, step 45° left or right — never straight back. Mix punches, switch kicks, elbows, jumping knees freely. Bag swinging back = re-engage from an angle.

Main Lift 1
Romanian Deadlift — dumbbell or kettlebell
4 × 15 reps · 3 min rest between sets
▼
  • StanceFeet hip-width, dumbbells at front of thighs (or one heavy KB in two hands)
  • SetupSoft knee bend, brace core
  • MovementHinge from hips — push hips back, weights glide down close to thighs
  • Bottom positionStop at hamstring stretch — don't round lower back
  • ReturnDrive hips forward, squeeze glutes at top
  • Tempo2 sec descent · 1 sec pause at bottom · controlled return
RDL loads posterior chain without spinal compression. Single most valuable lift for your back history. Workout A is the higher priority if schedule collapses.

Single-side loading creates anti-rotation core challenge — useful for back protection. Builds shoulder stability that complements kickboxing demands.

Dumbbell Bench Press
4 × 15 reps · 3 min rest between sets
▼
  • SetupLie on bench, dumbbells at shoulder height, feet planted
  • ScapulaeRetracted and pinned to bench — "pull the bar apart" tension across chest

Main Lift 2
Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
4 × 15 reps each arm · 3 min rest between sets
▼
  • SetupOne knee on bench, same-side hand on bench, opposite leg planted, dumbbell hanging below shoulder
  • BackFlat back, neutral spine throughout

Rows hit more muscle per set than curls — lats, mid-traps, rhomboids, rear delts, plus biceps as secondary. Highest-leverage upper-body work for your posture profile.

Progression + Notes
Weight progression
  • Add 1.25–2.5 kg per dumbbell when all 4 × 15 feel like 7/10 effort or below for two sessions in a row
  • Quality of movement is the goal — not the number on the dumbbell
Rep scheme
  • Stay at 4 × 15. If you want a hypertrophy stimulus shift, drop to 4 × 10 heavier for a 4-week block, then return to 4 × 15
Form check
  • Film one set per workout per month. Watch at slow speed.
  • Watch for: hip shift in RDL · lower-back arch in press · elbow flare in bench · lat engagement in row
Avoid
  • Full-depth squats — knees + hip labral
  • Heavy back-loaded barbell squats — lower back
  • Conventional deadlift from the floor — lower back
  • Behind-the-neck press — shoulder
  • Heavy upright rows — shoulder impingement
  • Anything putting right hip into deep flexion under load
  • Sprint intervals on hard surfaces — knee
